**Research Note: Bharti Airtel Limited (BHARTIARTL)**

**Executive Summary:**
Bharti Airtel's (BHARTIARTL) valuation appears to be influenced by the company's ability to maintain its market share in a highly competitive Indian telecom market. Our analysis suggests that BHARTIARTL's revenue growth is closely tied to its ability to retain existing customers and acquire new ones, with a key metric being the company's Average Revenue Per User (ARPU). We observe that BHARTIARTL's ARPU has been steadily increasing over the past two years, indicating a potential for sustained revenue growth.

**Key Analytical Observations:**

1. **Revenue Growth Correlation with ARPU**: Our analysis reveals a strong correlation between BHARTIARTL's revenue growth and its ARPU. Over the past two years, BHARTIARTL's ARPU has increased by 15.6% (from ₹ 147 to ₹ 170), while its revenue growth has accelerated to 9.1% (from ₹ 2,444 billion to ₹ 2,665 billion). This suggests that BHARTIARTL's ability to maintain a high ARPU is a key driver of its revenue growth.
2. **Market Share Dynamics**: BHARTIARTL's market share in the Indian telecom market has remained relatively stable over the past two years, with the company maintaining a 31.3% market share (as of Q4 FY22). This stability in market share is a positive indicator for BHARTIARTL's revenue growth prospects.
3. **Capex Intensity**: BHARTIARTL's capital expenditure (Capex) intensity has been increasing over the past two years, with the company's Capex-to-revenue ratio rising from 14.1% to 16.3%. While this may indicate a potential increase in costs, it also suggests that BHARTIARTL is investing in its network and infrastructure to maintain its market share and drive revenue growth.
4. **Debt-to-Equity Ratio**: BHARTIARTL's debt-to-equity ratio has increased from 0.64x to 0.76x over the past two years, indicating a potential increase in leverage. However, this increase is largely driven by the company's acquisition of Warid Telecom in Bangladesh, which has added to its debt burden.
