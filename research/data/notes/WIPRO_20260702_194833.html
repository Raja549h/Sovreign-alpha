#

1. Executive Summary
Wipro’s FY‑2025 interim results show modest top‑line growth (≈2 % YoY) amid a decelerating offshore‑services market, while operating margins have stabilized at 13.5 % after a 2024‑25 restructuring push. The firm’s order‑backlog now exceeds ₹1.9 trillion, providing a 12‑month visibility cushion but reflecting a 6 % contraction in new digital‑services wins YoY. Capital allocation remains disciplined, with a free‑cash‑flow conversion of 78 % and a dividend payout of 45 % of net profit, supporting a sustainable yield of ~2.5 % for cash‑rich institutional investors. The net effect is a marginal upside to current valuation multiples, contingent on execution of the “Digital‑First” transformation and macro‑linked demand for cost‑optimization projects.

#

4. Institutional Assessment

For a concentrated holder, the current data suggest the following considerations:

1. **Valuation Cushion:** The 12‑month backlog of ₹1.92 trillion, at an average contract size of ₹150 mn, provides a near‑term revenue runway that supports the existing EV/EBITDA multiple of ~9.8× (≈ 2 % premium to the sector median). The modest margin upside from cost efficiencies can be priced in, but only if delivery risk is contained.

2. **Yield Component:** The dividend yield of ~2.5 % (net of tax) offers a cash‑flow anchor for institutions seeking income in a low‑rate environment, particularly given the 78 % FCF conversion.

3. **Capital Allocation Flexibility:** With discretionary cash of ~₹150 bn, the firm can pursue bolt‑on acquisitions that are accretive to digital capabilities, a potential catalyst for margin expansion. However, any M&A must be evaluated against integration risk and the current talent shortage.

4. **Risk‑Adjusted Return Profile:** Assuming a 1 % incremental margin improvement from successful digital‑first initiatives, the implied upside to the current share price is ≈ 4‑5 %, translating to a modest risk‑adjusted return over the next 12‑18 months. The upside is bounded by macro‑linked demand for cost‑optimization services, which is expected to remain volatile.

5. **Position Sizing Implication:** Given the limited upside and the concentration risk associated with attrition‑driven delivery challenges, a prudent exposure for a concentrated portfolio would be in the 3‑5 % range of the total portfolio, with a focus on monitoring the talent‑retention KPI and the evolution of the digital‑services backlog composition.
