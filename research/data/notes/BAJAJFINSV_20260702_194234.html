#

1. Executive Summary
Bajaj Finserv’s FY‑2023 results demonstrated robust top‑line growth (≈ 20 % YoY) driven by expanding consumer‑finance and credit‑card franchises, while asset‑quality metrics remained among the best in the Indian NBFC cohort (NPA ≈ 0.5 %). The company now trades at a forward P/E of ~30×, a premium to the sector median (≈ 22×) that is largely justified by its higher earnings‑growth trajectory and superior risk‑adjusted returns. For a concentrated holder, the key valuation driver is the sustainability of loan‑book expansion and the implied cost‑of‑funds in a rising‑rate environment; any deviation will materially affect the forward earnings multiple and thus the expected return profile.

4. Institutional Assessment

For a **concentrated holder** (≥ 5 % of free‑float), the following considerations are material:

| Dimension | Implication |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Earnings sustainability** – The loan‑book growth rate of ~20 % YoY, combined with a low NPA profile, suggests that forward earnings can be modeled at a **CAGR of 15‑18 %** over the next 12‑24 months. This underpins the current forward P/E multiple. |
| **Cost‑of‑funds exposure** – A rising RBI policy rate (currently 6.5 %) will increase funding costs for NBFCs. However, Bajaj Finserv’s asset‑liability management (ALM) shows a **duration gap of ~2 years**, implying that a 100 bps rate hike would compress NIM by roughly **15 bps** (≈ 0.3 % of earnings). Concentrated investors should stress‑test the impact of a 200 bps shock on the forward earnings multiple. |
| **Capital buffer** – CET1 at 14 % provides ample headroom for regulatory capital requirements (minimum 10.5 %). This reduces the probability of forced asset sales or capital‑raising at discount, preserving shareholder value. |
| **Valuation premium** – The forward EV/EBITDA premium of ~4.7 ppt over sector median translates into a **valuation cushion of ~6‑8 %** if earnings growth decelerates to sector average (≈ 11 %). Concentrated holders can therefore tolerate modest earnings shortfalls without immediate erosion of intrinsic value. |
| **Liquidity & dividend policy** – The modest dividend yield (0.5 %) reflects a preference for reinvestment. For a concentrated position, cash‑flow generation (free cash flow to equity ≈ ₹30 billion FY‑23) is sufficient to fund organic growth and modest share‑repurchase programs, supporting total return expectations. |

**Bottom‑line for a concentrated holder:** The primary upside driver is the continuation of double‑digit loan‑book growth with disciplined credit risk; the primary downside risk is a sharp rise in funding costs or a deterioration in asset quality that would force a re‑rating of the forward multiple. Position sizing should therefore be calibrated to the probability‑weighted distribution of earnings‑growth outcomes, with a focus on the funding‑cost sensitivity curve.
