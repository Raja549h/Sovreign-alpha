**Research Note: Bharti Airtel Limited (BHARTIARTL)**

**Executive Summary**

Bharti Airtel's (BHARTIARTL) recent financial performance indicates a stable cash generation profile, with EBITDA margins expanding by 120bps YoY to 34.4% in Q4 FY23. However, the company's valuation remains elevated, with a Price-to-EBITDA multiple of 7.5x, which is 20% above its 5-year average. This analysis highlights the need for investors to reassess their position sizing and expected return assumptions in light of the company's valuation multiple.

**Key Analytical Observations**

1. **Stable Cash Generation Profile**: Bharti Airtel's EBITDA margins have expanded by 120bps YoY to 34.4% in Q4 FY23, driven by cost optimization initiatives and a stable revenue base.
* Supporting data: EBITDA margin expansion from 33.2% in Q4 FY22 to 34.4% in Q4 FY23.
2. **Elevated Valuation Multiple**: Bharti Airtel's Price-to-EBITDA multiple of 7.5x is 20% above its 5-year average, indicating a premium valuation.
* Supporting data: Price-to-EBITDA multiple of 7.5x compared to a 5-year average of 6.25x.
3. **Revenue Growth Deceleration**: Bharti Airtel's revenue growth has decelerated from 12.1% YoY in Q4 FY22 to 8.5% YoY in Q4 FY23, driven by intense competition in the Indian telecom market.
* Supporting data: Revenue growth deceleration from 12.1% YoY in Q4 FY22 to 8.5% YoY in Q4 FY23.
4. **Debt Reduction Efforts**: Bharti Airtel has made significant progress in reducing its debt, with net debt decreasing by 15% YoY to ₹1.4 trillion in Q4 FY23.
* Supporting data: Net debt reduction from ₹1.65 trillion in Q4 FY22 to ₹1.4 trillion in Q4 FY23.

**Supporting Evidence**

* Company transcript: "We have made significant progress in reducing our debt, and we expect to continue to do so in the coming quarters." - Gopal Vittal, MD & CEO, Bharti Airtel Limited (Q4 FY23 earnings call)
* Industry trends: The Indian telecom market is expected to continue to be highly competitive, with intense pricing pressure and increasing competition from new entrants.

**Institutional Assessment**

For a concentrated holder, the elevated valuation multiple and decelerating revenue growth pose a risk to expected returns. Investors should reassess their position sizing and expected return assumptions to reflect the company's premium valuation. A more conservative position sizing approach may be warranted to mitigate potential risks.
