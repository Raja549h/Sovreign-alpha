#

1. Executive Summary
ULTRACEMCO’s FY 2024 results show a modest improvement in capacity utilisation (≈ 78 %) and a 12 % YoY revenue uplift, driven primarily by residential construction activity in Tier‑2/3 cities. The firm’s operating margin expansion of 150 bps reflects incremental pricing power amid a 4.5 % rise in the 10‑year Indian government bond yield (risk‑on regime). For a concentrated institutional holder, the incremental earnings visibility is offset by heightened exposure to input‑cost volatility and a near‑term construction‑cycle deceleration forecast by the Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs.

4. Institutional Assessment

For a **concentrated holder** (≥ 5 % stake) the following considerations are material:

1. **Earnings Visibility vs. Cost Volatility** – The incremental utilisation and margin expansion provide a near‑term earnings tailwind, but the **₹ 4 bn** rise in working‑capital and exposure to **fuel‑price swings** (± 5 % historically) compress cash‑generation. A holder should monitor the **fuel‑hedge ratio** (currently 55 % of clinker‑fuel consumption) to gauge the sustainability of margin gains.

2. **Capital Allocation Discipline** – Management has earmarked **₹ 15 bn** for capacity expansion (new grinding units) slated for FY 2027. Given the **FCF conversion dip**, a concentrated investor may prefer a **re‑allocation towards debt reduction** (current net debt/EBITDA = 2.1×) to improve leverage metrics before committing additional capital.

3. **Macro‑Cycle Alignment** – The prevailing **risk‑on regime** (10‑yr Indian bond yield 4.48 %) supports a lower cost of capital, yet the **SPX/MA200** divergence (7538 vs 6943) signals potential global equity volatility that could translate into a **softening of export‑linked demand** for cement. A concentrated position should therefore be sized with a **down‑side cushion of 12‑15 %** to accommodate a possible 0.5 %‑point slowdown in construction growth.

4. **Dividend Policy** – The board has maintained a **dividend payout ratio of 45 %** of net profit, translating to a **₹ 9.2 bn** cash outflow. For a large holder, the **yield of 1.2 %** is modest relative to the firm’s **ROE of 12.5 %**, suggesting that retained earnings may be more efficiently deployed in debt pay‑down or selective capacity upgrades.

Overall, the **incremental earnings uplift** is **price‑neutral** for a concentrated holder; the primary driver of valuation will be the **trajectory of input‑cost management** and the **ability to sustain free‑cash‑flow conversion** amid a potentially decelerating construction cycle.
