#

1. Executive Summary
Apollo Hospitals continues to consolidate its leadership in premium tertiary care, posting a 12 % YoY revenue increase in FY 2025 (INR 23.8 bn) while maintaining an operating margin of 15.3 %. The firm’s balance sheet remains resilient, with net debt‑to‑EBITDA at 2.1×, but capital intensity is rising as a result of a INR 4.2 bn rollout of specialty centres. Macro‑level risk‑on conditions (10‑yr Indian government bond yield 4.48 % and SPX > MA200) support a stable cost‑of‑capital environment, yet the sector’s regulatory exposure and competitive pressure from emerging private‑chain hospitals warrant a calibrated sizing for any concentrated exposure.

4. Institutional Assessment

For a **concentrated holder** (≥ 5 % of free‑float) the following considerations are material:

| Dimension | Implication |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Valuation Sensitivity** | The incremental revenue from Tier‑2/3 expansion (≈ INR 1.7 bn FY 2025) translates to an incremental EV of ~ INR 9 bn (assuming 5.3× FY 2025 EBITDA). A 10 % deviation in the assumed growth trajectory would shift intrinsic value by ± INR 0.9 bn, i.e., ~ 0.8 % of market cap. |
| **Cost of Capital** | With the 10‑yr Indian sovereign yield at 4.48 % and a risk‑on equity risk premium of ~ 5.5 % (derived from SPX‑MA200 spread), the implied WACC is ~ 9.0 %. The current share price (₹1,120) reflects a forward‑looking P/EV of ~ 1.2×, indicating modest upside headroom under a base‑case scenario. |
| **Liquidity & Impact** | Average daily turnover for APOLLOHOSP is ~ 0.8 % of free‑float (≈ ₹1.5 bn). A 5 % position would represent ~ 6 days of volume, implying that any sizable rebalancing could exert price pressure. |
| **Regulatory Tail Risk** | The draft pricing ceiling could compress operating margin by up to 0.5 pp if fully implemented, reducing FY 2026 EBITDA by ~ INR 0.2 bn. For a concentrated holder, this translates to a potential EV reduction of ~ INR 1 bn, which is material relative to the position size. |
| **Strategic Leverage** | The declining net‑debt‑to‑EBITDA ratio improves financial flexibility, allowing the firm to fund further expansion without dilutive financing, which is favorable for a long‑term holder seeking stable cash‑flow generation. |

Overall, the data suggest **stable earnings growth with modest margin expansion**, offset by a **regulatory headwind** that could modestly curb upside. A concentrated holder should therefore calibrate position size to reflect a **risk‑adjusted return expectation of ~ 7‑8 % IRR**, assuming the current trajectory and no adverse regulatory shock.
