#

1. Executive Summary
Asian Paints continues to demonstrate resilient top‑line growth (≈ 12 % YoY in FY‑24) and incremental margin expansion (EBITDA margin up 0.8 pp to 21.5 %). The firm’s operating cash conversion remains robust (> 85 % of EBITDA), underpinning a modest leverage profile (net‑debt/EBITDA ≈ 0.4×). The combination of a durable domestic décor market, a disciplined capex cycle, and a stable dividend payout (≈ 55 % of net profit) suggests that a concentrated institutional holder should calibrate forward‑looking return expectations primarily on the incremental earnings yield rather than on headline growth alone.

4. Institutional Assessment

For a **concentrated holder**, the primary valuation lever is the **earnings yield premium** (≈ 1.4 pp over the NIFTY‑50 cost of capital). Given the firm’s **high cash conversion** and **low leverage**, the incremental yield is likely to be realized through **steady dividend payouts** (FY‑24 dividend per share ₹ 12.5, payout ≈ 55 % of net profit) and **modest earnings accretion** rather than aggressive expansion.

*Implications for position sizing*:
- **Return expectation** should be anchored to the **earnings yield** (≈ 6.8 %) plus a modest upside from margin improvement (≈ 0.5 % p.a.).
- **Capital allocation** flexibility suggests that a portion of the holding can be earmarked for **share‑repurchase** or **special dividend** if cash generation exceeds the 85 % threshold consistently over two fiscal years.
- **Liquidity profile** (average daily turnover ≈ 0.6 % of free‑float) supports a **10‑15 % portfolio exposure** for a concentrated fund without materially impacting market depth.
