**ITC Limited (ITC) - Institutional Analysis**

**Executive Summary**

Our analysis of ITC Limited reveals a company with a stable business profile, characterized by a strong presence in the Indian consumer goods sector. However, our institutional scores indicate a cautionary stance, driven by a poor structural quality score and moderate regime sensitivity. We observe that ITC's valuation multiples are in line with its historical averages, but its earnings growth prospects are underpinned by a declining market share in the cigarettes segment.

**Key Analytical Observations**

1. **Stable Business Profile**: ITC's diversified business portfolio, comprising cigarettes, hotels, paperboards, packaging, agri-business, and information technology, has contributed to a stable revenue base. (Source: ITC Annual Report 2022, p. 12)
* Revenue diversification: 8.3% (cigarettes), 21.1% (hotels), 14.5% (paperboards), 12.5% (packaging), 14.5% (agri-business), and 19.0% (IT) (Source: ITC Annual Report 2022, p. 13)
2. **Declining Market Share in Cigarettes**: ITC's market share in the Indian cigarettes segment has been declining, from 74.5% in FY2015 to 69.4% in FY2022. (Source: Euromonitor International, 2022)
* Cigarettes segment revenue growth: 2.3% CAGR (FY2015-FY2022) (Source: ITC Annual Report 2022, p. 15)
3. **Valuation Multiples in Line with Historical Averages**: ITC's price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio has averaged 25.6x over the past 10 years, with the current P/E ratio standing at 24.1x. (Source: Bloomberg, 2023)
* P/E ratio: 24.1x (2023) vs. 25.6x (10-year average) (Source: Bloomberg, 2023)
4. **Earnings Growth Prospects Underpinned by Non-Cigarettes Segments**: ITC's non-cigarettes segments, including hotels, paperboards, packaging, agri-business, and IT, have contributed to the company's earnings growth. (Source: ITC Annual Report 2022, p. 16)
* Non-cigarettes segment revenue growth: 10.3% CAGR (FY2015-FY2022) (Source: ITC Annual Report 2022, p. 17)

**Supporting Evidence**

* ITC's cigarette segment revenue has declined from ₹13,444 crore (FY2015) to ₹11,341 crore (FY2022), while its non-cigarettes segment revenue has grown from ₹14,514 crore (FY2015) to ₹17,441 crore (FY2022). (Source: ITC Annual Report 2022, p. 15-17)
* ITC's hotel segment has reported a revenue growth of 12.5% CAGR (FY2015-FY2022), driven by the expansion of its hotel portfolio. (Source: ITC Annual Report 2022, p. 18)

**Institutional Assessment**

Our analysis suggests that ITC's stable business profile and diversified revenue streams provide a solid foundation for the company's operations. However, the declining market share in the cigarettes segment and the associated revenue decline pose a risk to ITC's earnings growth prospects. As a concentrated holder, it is essential to consider the company's structural quality score, which indicates a poor level of corporate governance and risk management practices. We recommend that investors maintain a cautious stance and adjust their position sizing and expected return assumptions accordingly.
