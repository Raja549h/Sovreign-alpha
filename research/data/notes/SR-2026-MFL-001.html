4 — Central Observation

Gold price appreciation is masking a 50bps NIM compression and a 6.7 percentage-point decline in capital adequacy, while the non-gold segment — growing 172% YoY — carries a credit cost of 4.1% that is subsidised by gold loan profits and invisible at the consolidated level.

Three data points support this:

  • NIM fell from 10.8% to 10.3% (FY24 to FY25) despite gold prices rising and AUM growing 37%. Interest expense grew 36.9% vs interest income growth of 34.2% — the spread is narrowing at scale. (Source: Equitymaster)
  • CAR declined from 30.4% to 23.7% while AUM grew 37%. Capital buffer is eroding faster than asset growth. (Source: Equitymaster Key Ratio Analysis)
  • Non-gold credit cost is 4.1% vs gold loan GNPA of 0.0%. The non-gold book (personal loans, SME, home loans) is expanding 172% YoY but its credit risk is absorbed by gold loan profitability. (Source: IDBI Capital Q2FY25 call transcript)

7 — RBI Regulatory Framework

The RBI's June 2025 final guidelines on gold loans, effective 1 April 2026, introduce tiered LTV caps:

Loan SizeMax LTVImpact on Muthoot
Below Rs 2.5 lakh85%Majority of book (avg ticket Rs 88,000). No credit appraisal required.
Rs 2.5 – 5 lakh80%Mid-ticket segment. Moderate impact.
Above Rs 5 lakh75%High-value schemes (Rs 5 Cr). Effective LTV constrained to ~63-64% on bullet repayment at 17-18% rates.

Source: RBI Monetary Policy Review (6 June 2025), Business Standard; EY Impact Assessment (Feb 2026).

The LTV must now be calculated on total repayment due at maturity, not the disbursed amount. This closes a previous monitoring gap and effectively reduces the usable LTV on bullet loans by 10-12 percentage points for high-ticket loans.

8 — Competitive Positioning

Muthoot Finance is the largest pure-play gold loan NBFC in India with 7,568 branches (FY26). Its nearest competitor, Manappuram Finance, operates approximately 3,000 branches. Muthoot's gold loan AUM of Rs 1,65,030 Cr (consolidated, FY26) represents approximately 45-50% of the organised gold loan market.

The company has been classified in the RBI's Upper Layer NBFC category for four consecutive years, subjecting it to bank-like regulatory scrutiny.

9 — Framework Note

What would change the assessment:

  • NIM stabilisation or expansion over two consecutive quarters
  • CAR stabilisation above 25% with continued AUM growth
  • Non-gold segment credit cost declining below 3.0%
  • Gold loan GNPA remaining at 0.0% through a gold price correction of 15%+
  • Branch productivity (AUM per branch) maintaining Rs 31 Cr+ (FY26 standalone peak)

What would worsen the assessment:

  • Gold price decline of 15%+ triggering LTV breaches and forced auctions
  • Non-gold credit cost exceeding 5.0%
  • CAR falling below 20%
  • RBI further tightening LTV caps or restricting bullet repayments
  • Geographic concentration losses from South India economic slowdown
