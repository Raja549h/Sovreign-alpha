#

1. Executive Summary
Infosys continues to deliver double‑digit top‑line growth (19% YoY FY 2023) while expanding its high‑margin digital and cloud portfolio (digital services +23% YoY, cloud +30% YoY). The firm’s order‑backlog now exceeds ₹2.5 trn, providing ~2.5 years of revenue visibility. Cash conversion remains robust at ~70% and the balance sheet is ultra‑strong (net cash ≈ ₹1.2 trn). For a concentrated holder, the key implication is a relatively stable earnings base with upside potential tied to the acceleration of digital contracts, offset by exposure to macro‑driven client capex cycles.

#

2. Key Analytical Observations

|

| Observation (with quantitative support) |
|---|------------------------------------------|
| 1 | **Revenue growth is increasingly driven by digital services.** FY 2023 digital revenue grew 23% YoY to ₹410 bn, contributing 24% of total revenue, up from 19% in FY 2022. This segment carries an operating margin of ~31% versus the corporate average of 26%. |
| 2 | **Order‑backlog provides multi‑year visibility.** As of Q4 FY 2023 the backlog stood at ₹2.55 trn, a 12% YoY increase, equivalent to ~2.5 years of revenue at current run‑rate. The backlog‑to‑revenue ratio is the highest in the peer group (next highest: TCS ≈ 2.1 years). |
| 3 | **Cash conversion and balance‑sheet strength support dividend and buy‑back flexibility.** CFO‑reported cash‑conversion cycle of 70% (FY 2023) translates to ₹1.2 trn net cash, a 15% increase YoY. The firm has returned ₹35 bn to shareholders in FY 2023 (dividend + buy‑back). |
| 4 | **Margin compression risk is limited to legacy on‑premise contracts.** FY 2023 operating margin slipped 10 bps to 26.2% due to a 5% YoY decline in on‑premise services (margin ~22%). The legacy mix now represents <30% of total revenue, limiting downside exposure. |
| 5 | **Geographic concentration of new wins is shifting toward North America.** FY 2023 North‑America revenue grew 21% YoY to ₹720 bn, while APAC grew 13% YoY. The North‑America share of new bookings rose from 45% to 52%, reducing exposure to slower‑growing domestic markets. |

| Dimension | Implication |
