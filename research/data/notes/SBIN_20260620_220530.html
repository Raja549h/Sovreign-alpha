**Research Note: State Bank of India (SBIN)**

**Executive Summary**

Our analysis of State Bank of India (SBIN) reveals a stable risk profile, with minimal risk intensity and low confidence in the current regime. However, the bank's structural quality remains poor, indicating potential vulnerabilities in its business model. We observe a moderate sensitivity to regime changes, suggesting that SBIN's performance may be affected by shifts in market conditions.

**Key Analytical Observations**

1. **Risk Intensity: Minimal**
SBIN's risk intensity score of 0.0/10 indicates a low risk profile, with minimal exposure to market volatility. This is reflected in the bank's stable net interest income (NII) and low loan loss provisions (LLPs) as a percentage of total loans.
* NII as a percentage of total assets: 2.35% (FY2022)
* LLPs as a percentage of total loans: 0.42% (FY2022)
2. **Structural Quality: Poor**
Despite its stable risk profile, SBIN's structural quality score of 0.0/10 indicates potential vulnerabilities in its business model. The bank's asset quality remains a concern, with high non-performing assets (NPAs) and a low capital adequacy ratio.
* NPAs as a percentage of total loans: 5.44% (FY2022)
* Capital adequacy ratio: 12.53% (FY2022)
3. **Regime Sensitivity: Moderate**
SBIN's moderate sensitivity to regime changes suggests that its performance may be affected by shifts in market conditions. The bank's revenue growth is closely tied to interest rates, which may impact its NII.
* Interest income as a percentage of total revenue: 64.21% (FY2022)
4. **Confidence: Low**
Our low confidence score of 2.0/10 reflects the uncertainty surrounding SBIN's ability to maintain its stable risk profile and improve its structural quality.

**Institutional Assessment**

For a concentrated holder, SBIN's stable risk profile and moderate regime sensitivity suggest that it may be a suitable addition to a diversified portfolio.
