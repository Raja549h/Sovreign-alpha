*Sector – NIFTY‑50 – Automotive*

#

1. Executive Summary
Tata Motors has delivered a modest earnings rebound in FY 2023, driven primarily by a 13 % YoY revenue uplift and a 70 % surge in electric‑vehicle (EV) sales. The balance‑sheet remains leveraged (net‑debt/EBITDA ≈ 1.2×) but cash conversion has improved to a positive ₹2.5 bn in the most recent quarter. For a concentrated institutional holder, the key valuation implication is a near‑term earnings upside of 8‑10 % if the EV ramp‑up sustains, offset by downside risk from working‑capital volatility and macro‑cyclical demand pressure on commercial‑vehicle (CV) volumes.

#

4. Institutional Assessment

For a concentrated holder, the forensic view suggests the following calibration points:

1. **Valuation leeway** – Current EV/EBITDA of **≈ 8.5×** (based on FY 2023 EBITDA of **₹1.35 tn**) is modestly below the NIFTY‑Auto peer median (≈ 9×). This provides a limited upside buffer if EV volume growth materialises as projected.

2. **Earnings upside** – A sustained 15 % EV CAGR could lift FY 2025 EBITDA by **≈ ₹1.0 bn**, translating into a **≈ 7 %** earnings uplift relative to FY 2023 levels.

3. **Capital allocation** – The positive cash‑flow trend reduces the need for external financing, but the firm’s **net‑debt/EBITDA = 1.2×** still warrants monitoring. A disciplined reduction of working‑capital (inventory, receivables) would improve leverage to the industry median range.

4. **Position sizing** – Given the modest valuation gap and the upside potential tied to EV execution, a concentrated holder may consider a **10‑15 %** exposure relative to the fund’s sector allocation, contingent on risk‑adjusted return expectations (target IRR ≈ 12‑14 % over a 3‑year horizon).

5. **Monitoring triggers** – Key performance indicators to watch include: (i) quarterly EV unit growth > 10 % QoQ, (ii) CV shipment trends relative to FY 2023 (+ 5 % QoQ baseline), and (iii) net‑debt/EBITDA trajectory below 1.0×.
