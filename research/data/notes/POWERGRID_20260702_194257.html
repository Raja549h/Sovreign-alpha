#

1. Executive Summary
Power Grid Corp (PGC) continues to operate as the backbone of India’s transmission network, delivering a stable cash‑flow profile that is increasingly insulated from short‑term macro volatility. The latest tariff revisions (FY‑2025) have lifted the weighted average revenue per kVA‑km by ~3 % YoY, while the company’s debt‑to‑EBITDA ratio remains at 2.1×, well below the sector median of 2.8×. In a risk‑on environment—evidenced by a 10‑year sovereign yield of 4.48 % and the S&P 500 trading ~9 % above its 200‑day moving average—POWERGRID’s dividend yield of 5.2 % and its low‑beta profile suggest a modest contribution to portfolio return expectations, with upside potential primarily linked to incremental tariff uplift and efficient capital deployment.

4. Institutional Assessment

For a concentrated holder, the primary valuation driver is the **steady, regulated cash‑flow stream** rather than growth acceleration. The incremental tariff uplift (≈3 % YoY) adds ~INR 50 bn of incremental EBITDA, which, when capitalised at a sector‑appropriate cost of capital (≈7.5 % for a high‑quality utility), yields an incremental enterprise value of roughly INR 670 bn. This translates to a **potential upside of 4–5 % on current market capitalisation**, assuming the market fully prices the tariff revision.

The **low leverage** and **strong dividend yield** provide a defensive cushion; in a scenario where equity markets revert to risk‑off (10‑yr yield >5 %), the impact on PGC’s cost of capital is muted relative to higher‑beta peers, preserving the dividend‑adjusted return profile.

Consequently, a concentrated position should be sized to reflect **expected return contribution of 5–6 % annualised (dividend yield plus modest earnings growth)**, with a **risk buffer** calibrated to the identified regulatory and macro‑interest‑rate vectors. Position sizing can be increased relative to peers with higher beta, but the ceiling for upside remains limited by the regulated nature of the business.
