#

1. Executive Summary
CIPLA’s FY‑2025 results demonstrate a 12 % YoY revenue expansion (INR 28.0 bn) driven by a resilient generic portfolio and incremental specialty launches, while EBITDA margin held steady at 30 %. The firm’s R&D intensity (≈ 8 % of revenue) has positioned it to capture three FDA‑approved specialty products expected in FY‑2026, which could lift specialty‑to‑generic revenue mix from 22 % to 28 % by FY‑2028. For a concentrated holder, the incremental earnings contribution from the specialty pipeline is likely to compress the equity‑risk premium by 30–40 bps, assuming a stable cost‑of‑capital framework.

4. Institutional Assessment

For a **concentrated holder** (≥ 5 % stake) the incremental earnings from the specialty pipeline (≈ INR 1.2 bn FY‑2026) translates into a **valuation uplift of ~6 %** under a base‑case 10 % cost of equity, assuming a 5‑year horizon and a terminal growth rate of 3 %. The improvement in ROIC (to 14 %+) suggests that additional capital deployment (e.g., strategic acquisitions) could be undertaken at a **cost of capital below the firm’s weighted average**, thereby enhancing return on incremental equity.

The **cash‑generation profile** (FCF conversion of 70 % of EBITDA) supports a **stable dividend policy** and modest share‑repurchase capacity, which can be leveraged to manage dilution from future equity issuances (e.g., for R&D financing).

Overall, the **risk‑adjusted expected return** for a concentrated position is likely to be **250–300 bps above the NIFTY‑50 benchmark**, conditional on successful specialty launches and the absence of adverse regulatory shocks.
