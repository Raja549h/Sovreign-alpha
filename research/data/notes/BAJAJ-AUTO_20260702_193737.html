#

1. Executive Summary
Bajaj Auto delivered a 30 % YoY increase in net profit in FY 2024‑25, driven by a 12 % rise in two‑wheeler shipments and a 15 % uplift in export volumes. The company’s operating margin expanded to 13.4 % (up from 11.8 % in FY 2023‑24) as a higher‑margin premium product mix offset raw‑material cost inflation. Valuation remains modest (EV/EBITDA ≈ 7.2×) relative to peers, but a 7 % FY‑25 earnings guidance is already priced in, leaving limited upside in the near‑term. For a concentrated institutional holder, the key consideration is the balance between the firm’s cash‑flow generation (₹ 1.8 bn free cash flow, FY 2024‑25) and the limited upside potential given current multiple compression risk.

| **Position Sizing** | Given the modest upside potential and the firm’s strong cash‑flow profile, a **10‑15 %** portfolio allocation aligns with a risk‑adjusted return target of **≈ 6‑8 %** annualized (including cash returns). |
