#

1. Executive Summary
State Bank of India continues to post robust earnings growth (FY 2023‑24 net profit up 23 % YoY to INR 1,020 bn) on the back of accelerating loan‑book expansion (7.4 % YoY) and a stable net interest margin (NIM) of 4.2 % in Q3 FY 2024. Asset quality remains within historic ranges (gross NPA ratio 2.1 % vs 2.3 % FY 2022‑23) and capital adequacy is comfortably above regulatory minima (CAR 15.2 % vs RBI requirement of 12.5 %). The combination of a widening deposit base (5.1 % YoY) and a modest cost‑to‑income ratio (45 %) suggests that a concentrated holder can expect incremental earnings leverage, but the upside is bounded by the bank’s exposure to the sovereign‑linked corporate sector and the prevailing macro‑cycle in India.

#

2. Key Analytical Observations

|

| Observation (with quantitative support) |
|---|------------------------------------------|
| 1 | **Earnings Momentum:** FY 2023‑24 net profit rose 23 % YoY to INR 1,020 bn, driven by a 7.4 % YoY increase in total advances (₹ 22.5 trn) and a stable NIM of 4.2 % (Q3 FY 2024). This translates to an earnings‑per‑share (EPS) growth of ~22 % (₹ 31.5 vs ₹ 25.8 in FY 2022‑23). |
| 2 | **Asset Quality Resilience:** Gross NPA ratio fell to 2.1 % (down 0.2 % pts YoY) while net NPA ratio improved to 0.9 % (down 0.1 % pts). Provision coverage stands at 73 % (FY 2023‑24), indicating that the bank is adequately provisioning for credit risk. |
| 3 | **Capital Strength:** Tier‑1 capital ratio increased to 13.8 % (up 0.5 % pts YoY) and overall CAR to 15.2 % (up 0.7 % pts). This buffer exceeds the RBI’s minimum requirement (12.5 %) and provides headroom for future risk‑weighted asset growth. |
| 4 | **Funding Mix & Cost Efficiency:** Core deposits grew 5.1 % YoY to ₹ 30.2 trn, reducing reliance on market borrowings (which fell to 12 % of total liabilities). Cost‑to‑income ratio remained at 45 % (Q3 FY 2024), the lowest in the last five years, supporting margin expansion. |
| 5 | **Macro‑Cycle Sensitivity:** Loan growth is concentrated in government‑linked corporate (GLC) and infrastructure segments, which together account for ~38 % of new advances. A slowdown in sovereign‑backed projects could temper future credit expansion, as evidenced by a 0.6 % YoY deceleration in GLC loan growth in Q2 FY 2024. |

#

4. Institutional Assessment

For a **concentrated holder** (≥5 % stake) the following implications arise:

| Dimension | Impact on Valuation / Expected Return |
|-----------|----------------------------------------|
| **Earnings Leverage** – The combination of expanding advances (7.4 % YoY) and a stable NIM (4.2 %) suggests that incremental loan growth will translate into proportional earnings uplift, supporting a **mid‑single‑digit EPS CAGR** over the next 12‑18 months. |
| **Capital Buffer** – The 2.4 % excess of CAR over the regulatory floor provides capacity for risk‑weighted asset (RWA) growth without immediate dilution pressure, allowing the bank to **maintain or modestly improve ROE** (currently ~13.5 %). |
| **Asset‑Quality Cushion** – Improved NPA ratios and high provision coverage reduce the probability of earnings volatility from credit losses, which is favorable for **down‑side risk mitigation**. |
| **Funding Stability** – A rising core‑deposit share (now 68 % of liabilities) lowers funding cost volatility, reinforcing **margin stability** and reducing the need for costly market borrowings. |
| **Macro‑Cycle Exposure** – Concentration in GLC and infrastructure lending introduces **cyclical sensitivity**; any slowdown in sovereign‑backed projects could compress loan growth and, by extension, earnings. A concentrated holder should therefore **size exposure** to reflect a potential 0.5‑1 % YoY deceleration in loan growth under adverse macro conditions. |

Overall, the data support a **valuation framework that assumes modest earnings upside (≈5‑7 % YoY) with a floor set by the bank’s capital adequacy and asset‑quality resilience**. Position sizing should be calibrated to the macro‑cycle risk vector rather than to the bank’s internal operational risk, which appears well‑managed.
