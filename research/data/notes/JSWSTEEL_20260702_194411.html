#

1. Executive Summary
JSW Steel has entered FY‑24 with a 15 % YoY increase in realized steel prices, a 10 % improvement in capacity utilisation and a net‑debt/EBITDA ratio of 2.0×, reflecting a modestly tighter balance‑sheet relative to FY‑23. The firm’s 13 % FY‑24 capex programme is directed toward green‑steel conversion and incremental capacity (≈1 MT), which should sustain margin expansion if demand remains resilient. For a concentrated institutional holder, the current valuation embeds a 12 % CAGR earnings trajectory; the key upside/downside hinges on steel‑price dynamics and the execution of the green‑steel roadmap.

2. Key Analytical Observations

|

| Observation (with quantitative backing) |
|---|------------------------------------------|
| **1** | **Steel‑price environment is favourable:** The Indian Steel Price Index (ISPI) closed FY‑24 at 1,210 points, a 15 % YoY rise (vs. 1,050 points in FY‑23). This translates into a 12 % uplift in average realised price per tonne (₹5,400 / t vs. ₹4,800 / t FY‑23). |
| **2** | **Capacity utilisation has risen to 80 %:** Total steel production reached 20.9 MT (up 9 % YoY). Utilisation moved from 71 % in FY‑23 to 80 % in FY‑24, narrowing the gap to the 85 % historical optimum. |
| **3** | **Debt metrics remain manageable:** Net debt stood at ₹71 bn (FY‑24 Q4), while EBITDA was ₹35 bn, giving a net‑debt/EBITDA of 2.0× (down from 2.3× in FY‑23). Cash‑flow from operations was ₹28 bn, sufficient to cover 70 % of scheduled debt service. |
| **4** | **Capex is directed toward green‑steel and incremental capacity:** FY‑24 capex of ₹13 bn (≈7.6 % of revenue) is allocated 55 % to a 1 MT green‑steel line (electric arc furnace) and 45 % to ancillary projects (logistics, automation). The green‑steel line is expected to be operational Q3 FY‑25, with an anticipated 300 bps margin uplift. |
| **5** | **Export exposure is modest but growing:** Export sales rose to 30 % of total revenue (₹51 bn) in FY‑24, up from 24 % in FY‑23. The export basket is weighted toward high‑margin specialty steel, which benefitted from a 10 % YoY price increase in the EU market. |
