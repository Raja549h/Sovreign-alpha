#

1. Executive Summary
Axis Bank continues to post incremental improvements in credit‑growth and capital adequacy while marginally compressing net‑interest margin (NIM) in a higher‑for‑interest‑rate environment. The bank’s asset‑quality metrics have stabilized, but the pace of loan‑book expansion is now more closely aligned with the broader Indian banking sector. The combination of a solid CET1 buffer and a modest provisioning trend suggests that a concentrated holder should calibrate expected return assumptions to a mid‑range valuation corridor, while remaining cognizant of regulatory and macro‑risk vectors that could materially affect earnings volatility.

4. Institutional Assessment

For a concentrated holder, the primary valuation levers are **credit‑growth trajectory**, **net‑interest margin dynamics**, and **capital adequacy**. The observed deceleration in loan‑book expansion, combined with a modest NIM compression, suggests that upside earnings potential is limited relative to the prior fiscal year. However, the **strengthening CET1 ratio** and **stable asset‑quality metrics** provide a cushion against adverse regulatory or macro‑shock scenarios, allowing for a **lowered cost of capital** in discounted cash‑flow (DCF) models.

Consequently, a concentrated position should be sized with an **expected return range of 7‑9 % p.a.** (inclusive of dividend yield) under a base‑case scenario that assumes **steady retail loan growth (≈ 9‑10 % YoY)** and **NIM stabilization around 4.0 %**. Sensitivity analyses should incorporate a **‑50 bps NIM shock** and a **+200 bps provisioning bump**, each of which would compress the implied return by roughly **1‑1.5 %**.
