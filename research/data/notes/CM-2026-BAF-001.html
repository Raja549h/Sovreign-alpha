Three Falsifiable Observations

Observation 1 — Credit Cost Trajectory

Bajaj Finance's credit cost has accelerated from 1.25% (FY23) to 2.05% (FY25) — a 64% increase over two years — while management has guided for normalization at 1.7–1.8% for three consecutive quarters. The gap between guided and actual credit cost has widened every quarter since Q2FY25.

Falsifiable by: Credit cost falling below 1.70% for any two consecutive quarters in FY26.

Observation 2 — Valuation vs. Realized Returns

At the current P/BV of 4.6x, a reverse Gordon Growth Model implies a sustainable ROE of ~78%. The company's actual ROE is 17.4% and declining (peak: 22.5% in FY23). No Indian NBFC has sustained a ROE above 25% at this AUM scale (₹3.5L Cr+). The stock is priced for a level of profitability that has never existed.

Falsifiable by: ROE inflecting above 22% in any quarter of FY26, or P/BV de-rating below 3.5x.

Observation 3 — Funding Cost Outpacing Growth

Interest expense growth (32.3% YoY) has exceeded AUM growth (28.0% YoY) in FY25, creating negative operating leverage on the liability side. NIM has compressed 70 bps in two years (10.8% → 10.1%). If funding costs continue to rise faster than asset yields, each incremental rupee of AUM will be margin-dilutive.

Falsifiable by: NIM expanding above 10.5% in H1FY26 while AUM growth stays above 25%.
