*Sector: Consumer Goods*

#

1. Executive Summary
Hindustan Unilever Limited (HUL) delivered FY 2025‑26 revenue growth of **4.2 % YoY** (₹ 78.5 bn) on a stable underlying margin of **15.1 %**, reflecting resilient demand for essential FMCG categories despite a modest slowdown in discretionary spend. The company’s **share‑of‑wallet (SOW) expansion of 1.8 pp** in the oral care and home care segments underscores a continued ability to capture incremental volume in a saturated market. Capital efficiency remains high, with **ROCE of 22.4 %** and a **free cash flow conversion of 84 %**, supporting a disciplined dividend policy and modest cap‑ex outlays. For a concentrated institutional holder, the key valuation implication is a **low‑to‑moderate upside in earnings multiples** (≈ 0.8‑1.0 × FY 26E EBITDA) given the limited growth runway and the firm’s already premium pricing relative to peers.

4. Institutional Assessment

For a **concentrated holder** (≥ 5 % stake), the forensic analysis suggests the following positioning considerations:

| Consideration | Implication |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Earnings Growth Ceiling** – With FY 26E revenue growth projected at 3‑4 % and margin stability, incremental earnings are likely to be modest. A concentrated holder should calibrate expectations for **earnings‑driven upside** to **≤ 0.8 × FY 26E EBITDA**. |
| **Cash Flow Reliability** – High free‑cash‑flow conversion (84 %) and a disciplined dividend policy provide a **stable cash‑return stream**, supporting a **lower‑risk return component** in the portfolio. |
| **Share‑of‑Wallet Expansion** – The SOW gains in oral and home care translate into **higher contribution margins**; however, the absolute volume uplift is limited (≈ 2 % YoY). The holder may allocate **additional capital** only if **category‑specific pricing power** improves. |
| **Capital Allocation Discipline** – The ongoing share‑repurchase programme (₹ 1.5 bn) and modest cap‑ex (≈ ₹ 2.2 bn) indicate **limited reinvestment needs**, reducing the risk of **dilutive financing**. |
| **Valuation Headroom** – Current EV/EBITDA of 9.4× is only modestly below the sector average, implying **limited multiple expansion** potential. The holder should therefore **focus on cash‑flow yield** rather than multiple upside. |

Overall, the forensic view points to **steady, low‑volatility cash generation** with **constrained upside**. A concentrated position should be sized to reflect a **return profile anchored in dividend yield (≈ 5 % p.a.) and modest earnings growth**, rather than speculative multiple expansion.
