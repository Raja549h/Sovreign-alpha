**Executive Summary**

Page Industries Limited's (PAGEIND) valuation appears fragile, with a trailing P/BV of 27.8x and PE of 50.6x, implying a sustained growth assumption. However, the company's ROE has declined 1,140 bps over four periods, and the ROE trajectory suggests diminishing marginal returns on incremental capital employed. Furthermore, capex has exceeded PAT in FY25, resulting in negative free cash flow at the operating level. These factors contribute to an elevated risk profile, warranting caution in position sizing and expected return assumptions.

**Key Analytical Observations**

1. **ROE Decline and Diminishing Marginal Returns**: ROE has declined from 51.8% (FY24) to 35.6% (FY25), a 1,620 bps decline, despite PAT growing 28.1% to ₹7,291M. This suggests diminishing marginal returns on incremental capital employed.
2. **Capex Overshoot and Negative Free Cash Flow**: Capex of ₹7,957M in FY25 exceeds PAT of ₹7,291M, resulting in negative free cash flow at the operating level. This implies the company is borrowing or drawing down cash to fund expansion.
3. **Valuation Fragility**: The trailing P/BV of 27.8x and PE of 50.6x imply a sustained growth assumption, which may not be justified given the ROE decline and capex overshoot.
4. **Inventory Volatility and Working Capital Cycles**: Inventory days have swung significantly over the past few years, representing a substantial working capital risk. While FY25 improvement is positive, the volatility pattern suggests inventory management is not structurally optimised.
5. **Single-Brand Concentration Risk**: Page Industries derives ~100% of revenue from Jockey-branded products under an exclusive license from Jockey International (US). Any degradation in Jockey's global brand equity, license renegotiation friction, or brand-related litigation would have a direct, non-diversifiable revenue impact.
