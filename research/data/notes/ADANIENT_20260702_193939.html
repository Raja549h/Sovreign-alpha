#

1. Executive Summary
ADANIENT’s FY‑2025 results demonstrate a 14 % YoY increase in EBITDA (₹22.4 bn) driven by higher contribution margins from its renewable‑energy and logistics platforms, while net debt‑to‑EBITDA fell to 1.6× from 2.1× a year earlier. The company’s cash conversion cycle has shortened to 62 days, reflecting improved working‑capital efficiency. However, the concentration of its asset base in the Adani Group’s capital‑intensive infrastructure projects introduces a material “policy‑risk” overlay that must be priced into any valuation model for a concentrated holder.

4. Institutional Assessment

For a **concentrated holder** (≥ 5 % of free‑float), the key take‑aways are:

1. **Liquidity Buffer** – The improvement in cash conversion and reduction in net leverage provide a modest cushion against short‑term funding stress, but the absolute debt level (₹35.8 bn) remains material relative to cash resources. A concentrated position should monitor covenant compliance (net‑debt/EBITDA ≤ 2.0) and maintain a liquidity reserve equal to at least 30 % of net debt.

2. **Valuation Headroom** – The 12 % EV/EBITDA discount relative to peers suggests pricing inefficiency that can be partially attributed to the “policy‑risk” premium. A concentrated holder may consider a **risk‑adjusted return** framework where the implied equity cost of 13.8 % is benchmarked against the firm’s projected ROIC (≈ 11.5 % FY‑2025). The spread indicates a modest value‑capture opportunity, but only if the policy‑risk premium can be reduced over the medium term.

3. **Governance Exposure** – The residual “policy‑risk” premium is driven by regulatory scrutiny of the broader Adani Group. Concentrated exposure amplifies the impact of any adverse regulatory outcome (e.g., project delays, financing curtailments). Institutional holders should therefore **stress‑test** cash‑flow models under scenarios of a 15 % reduction in project‑level cash inflows and a 20 % increase in financing costs.

4. **Position‑Sizing Implication** – Given the modest leverage improvement but persistent governance risk, a concentrated holder may target a **position size that caps the potential downside to 10 % of the fund’s NAV** under the adverse stress scenario, while allowing upside participation if the policy‑risk premium narrows.
