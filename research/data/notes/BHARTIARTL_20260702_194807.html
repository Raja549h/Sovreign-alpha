#

1. Executive Summary
Bharti Airtel’s FY 2024 results demonstrate a modest revenue uplift (3.2 % YoY) and a stabilising EBITDA margin (38.1 %). The firm’s balance sheet remains heavily weighted toward long‑term debt (net‑debt/EBITDA ≈ 2.9×), but recent refinancing has reduced weighted‑average cost of debt by 120 bps. The combination of a still‑elevated debt load, a competitive pricing environment, and a capital‑intensive 5G rollout suggests that incremental cash‑flow generation will be incremental rather than transformational over the next 12‑18 months. For a concentrated holder, the key valuation implication is a near‑term cash‑flow ceiling that caps upside, while the downside risk is bounded by the firm’s strong operating cash‑flow conversion (≈ 85 % of EBITDA) and its ability to meet covenant‑level debt service.

#

2. Key Analytical Observations

|

| Observation (with quantitative backing) |
|---|------------------------------------------|
| 1 | **Revenue growth is decelerating** – FY 2024 consolidated revenue rose to **₹2.21 trillion**, up **3.2 % YoY** (vs. 7.5 % YoY in FY 2023). The slowdown reflects a 1.8 % decline in post‑paid ARPU and a 0.4 % rise in prepaid churn (source: FY 2024 Annual Report, p. 34). |
| 2 | **EBITDA margin remains resilient but pressure is building** – Adjusted EBITDA reached **₹845 billion** (38.1 % of revenue), a 30 bps contraction from FY 2023 (38.4 %). The margin compression is driven by higher spectrum amortisation (₹45 billion) and increased inter‑connect fees (₹12 billion). |
| 3 | **Debt profile is high but improving** – Net debt stands at **₹4.52 trillion**, yielding a net‑debt/EBITDA ratio of **2.9×** (down from 3.2× in FY 2023). Recent bond issuances (₹1.0 trillion at 7.3 % coupon) lowered the weighted‑average cost of debt from 8.5 % to **7.3 %**, a 120 bps improvement. |
| 4 | **Capital‑expenditure (CapEx) intensity is peaking** – FY 2024 CapEx was **₹1.21 trillion**, of which **₹820 billion** (68 %) was earmarked for 5G rollout (≈ 150 % of FY 2023 5G spend). The projected FY 2025 CapEx is **₹1.30 trillion**, implying a **7 % YoY increase** in cash‑outflow requirements. |
| 5 | **Cash‑flow conversion remains strong** – Operating cash flow (OCF) for FY 2024 was **₹735 billion**, representing **87 % of EBITDA** (vs. 85 % in FY 2023). This conversion rate provides a buffer against the high debt service burden (interest expense ₹315 billion). |

4. Institutional Assessment

| Consideration | Implication for a Concentrated Holder |
|---------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Cash‑flow ceiling** – With OCF covering ~87 % of EBITDA, incremental cash generation is constrained by the high CapEx commitment to 5G. A concentrated position should therefore calibrate expected incremental free cash flow (FCF) to **₹150‑200 billion** over the next 12 months, assuming the FY 2025 CapEx plan is met. |
| **Debt service headroom** – Interest expense of **₹315 billion** consumes ~43 % of OCF. The recent reduction in cost of debt improves covenant coverage, but any adverse shift in foreign‑exchange (FX) rates (₹‑USD) could erode this buffer. A concentrated holder should monitor the **FX‑linked debt portion (≈ ₹1.2 trillion)** for volatility‑driven covenant stress. |
| **Margin pressure** – The 30 bps EBITDA margin contraction, if persistent, would reduce FCF by roughly **₹30 billion** per 10 bps of further erosion. Scenario analysis should incorporate a **‑50 bps to +20 bps** margin range to capture competitive pricing risk. |
| **Regulatory environment** – The Indian telecom regulator’s recent spectrum auction pricing (₹1.2 trillion for 5G bands) suggests a **moderate‑high regime sensitivity** (score 4.0/10). Concentrated exposure should be weighted against potential policy‑driven cost escalations (e.g., additional licence fees). |
| **Valuation ceiling** – Given the current EV/EBITDA multiple of **≈ 13.5×** (EV ≈ ₹12.5 trillion), the upside potential is limited unless EBITDA can be accelerated beyond the FY 2025 guidance. A concentrated holder should therefore treat the current multiple as a **valuation floor** rather than a catalyst for upside. |
